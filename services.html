Services

Little Way Speech Therapy provides individualized, family-centered speech and language services for children across a variety of communication needs. Sessions are engaging, play-based, and thoughtfully tailored to support meaningful progress in everyday life.

Areas of Support

Speech Sound Disorders

Support for articulation and phonological development to improve speech clarity, confidence, and overall intelligibility.

Language Development

Building receptive and expressive language skills to strengthen understanding, vocabulary, sentence development, and functional communication.

Social Communication

Helping children develop skills for conversation, perspective-taking, play, and meaningful connection with others.

AAC Support

Guidance with augmentative and alternative communication, including device trials, funding support, programming, and implementation across environments.

Early Intervention

Play-based therapy for infants and toddlers focused on early communication, interaction, caregiver support, and developmental milestones.

Parent Coaching

Practical, easy-to-use strategies that help caregivers support communication growth during everyday routines and natural interactions.

What to Expect

Services typically begin with an initial consultation or evaluation to better understand your child’s strengths, needs, and goals. From there, therapy sessions are individualized and designed to build skills in a supportive, engaging environment. Collaboration with caregivers is prioritized throughout the process so strategies can carry over beyond sessions and into daily life.

Service Delivery Options

Services may be provided in the home, in natural environments, or through other individualized arrangements depending on your child’s needs, age, and goals. Private pay services are available for families seeking direct support, flexible scheduling, or consultation outside of school- or agency-based services.
